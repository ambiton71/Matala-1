8 FACTS ABOUT GRAPES

Grapes have been cultivated for thousands of years, with evidence of their cultivation dating back to ancient civilizations such as the Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans.

There are thousands of grape varieties grown worldwide, with the most common types being table grapes and wine grapes.

Grapes are rich in antioxidants, particularly resveratrol, which has been linked to various health benefits, including heart health and longevity.

Grapes come in various colors, including red, green, and purple. The color of the grape is determined by the grape's skin, not the pulp.

When grapes are dried, they become raisins. Raisins are a popular and nutritious snack.

The majority of grapes are used for wine production. Different grape varieties are cultivated for red and white wines.

Grapes are grown in many parts of the world, with notable wine-producing regions including Bordeaux in France and Mendoza in Argentina.

Grapes are used not only for eating and wine production but also in the production of grape juice, jams, jellies, and even certain types of brandy.
